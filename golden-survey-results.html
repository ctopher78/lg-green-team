Recommended Rollout Tiers

Tier 1 — Start Now

High interest • Low-medium cost • Simple compliance
Talks / Events / Clubs Outdoor Skills Repair Clinics Sewing / Fiber Arts

Talks and events provide foundational infrastructure every other activity shares — chairs, A/V, tables. Outdoor skills are nearly free to launch with strong, consistent community interest. Repair clinics and sewing both have solid demand, low compliance burden, and grow well incrementally.

Tier 2 — Plan & Fund

Strong passion signal • Medium investment • Manageable compliance
3D Printing / Design Painting / Printmaking Woodworking

3D Printing ranks #1 in top priorities despite modest overall interest — enthusiasts are highly motivated. Painting has a strong passion-to-cost ratio; watch supply burn. Woodworking has consistent demand and manageable buildout with the right safety investment (SawStop is non-negotiable).

Tier 3 — Long Game

High cost or compliance • Or weaker demand signal
Community Kitchen Ceramics Metalworking / Welding Small Business Dev

Community Kitchen is the most desired activity but carries the highest regulatory and capital bar — health dept certification, licensed HVAC/fire contractor, NSF equipment. Ceramics shows many curious but few committed (only 5 top-priority picks). Metalworking has the lowest passion signal and highest ongoing safety burden; revisit after woodshop is established. Small Business Dev absorbs into the events space.

What Already Exists in Golden

Before building something new, we mapped what already exists locally — and where the genuine gaps are

The survey tells us what Golden residents want. This analysis answers: what already exists in Golden proper? And where are the genuine gaps — activities the community wants, but nowhere currently offers? We researched every venue in Golden offering classes, creative access, or community programming. The picture is striking: five of the top-interest activities have zero local options, and no venue offers flexible drop-in creative or maker access outside of class time.

Programming Vision — Build What CCM Isn't

Four programming pillars grounded in survey data

Community Hub

Talks • Clubs • Skill swaps • Speaker series
  • Monthly speaker series — local experts, entrepreneurs, naturalists
  • Book club, film club, language exchange
  • Skill-swap nights ("I teach bread, you teach Spanish")
  • Open coffee mornings — no agenda, just neighbors
"I want a place where I can just walk in and something interesting is happening."

Kitchen & Food

Cooking series • Bread lab • Preserve & pickle • Community potluck
  • Weekly cooking classes by theme — 60% survey interest, zero local options
  • Bread Lab — sourdough, laminated doughs, naturally leavened
  • Preserve & Pickle — fermentation, canning, foraging
  • Monthly potluck — open to all, bring a dish
"A community kitchen would bring people together more than anything else in Golden."

Maker Core

3D printing • Laser cutting • Sewing • Open studio
  • Gateway classes — first session free, accessible pricing
  • Open studio hours every evening + weekend — just show up
  • Sewing circles, mending clinics, natural dye workshops
  • 3D design + print: beginner to advanced, project-based
"I just want somewhere I can work on projects without having to own every tool."

Outdoor & Repair

Gear repair • Fix-It Fridays • Outdoor skills
  • Fix-It Fridays — bring a broken thing, leave with it working
  • Gear repair clinics — pack stitching, boot resoling, bike tuning
  • Outdoor skills lite — navigation, knots, wilderness first aid basics
  • Bike maintenance nights — open to all experience levels
"I'd love a repair cafe — sustainable, practical, and a great way to meet people."

Feasibility & Path Forward

Phased Approach — Don't Build CCM in Golden. Build What CCM Isn't.

Phase 1 — Lean Launch (~50 members + grants)
  • Talks, events, repair clinics, sewing, 3D printing — achievable with $8–18k buildout
  • Target: $8–12k/mo gross at 50 members + 10 classes/mo + grants
  • Focus on community-first programming: talks, clubs, skill swaps, Fix-It Fridays
  • Nonprofit 501(c)(3) structure like Localworks is critical for grant eligibility and trust
Phase 2 — Investment Milestones (100+ members)
  • Community Kitchen — $60–100k buildout; requires health dept certification, licensed HVAC/fire contractor, NSF equipment. High reward: 60% survey interest, zero competition.
  • Woodshop — $80–150k; SawStop non-negotiable for safety. Strong demand (49%) with no local alternative.
Key Structural Principles
  • Community-first, tools-second — CCM has the tools; Golden needs the gathering place
  • Evening + weekend hours are mandatory — 68% of survey respondents require this
  • Drop-in access — 72% want no commitment required; structured only programming will fail
  • Financial accessibility — sliding scale, volunteer hours, subsidized memberships
  • Don't compete with CCM on equipment depth — partner, cross-promote, refer
